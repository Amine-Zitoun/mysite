How I Started:



So basically, I was a normal kid looking for a passion and sort of lost, until one of my cool friends, my best friend in particular, told me about coding. I wasn't motivated at the time, because I had considered it once before and didn't really like the idea, but together we motivated each other, and we eventually came to love it.

When I got home, I started learning C#, but I didn't really have a purpose at the time. I was told it was easy so I kinda got the hang of it, yet it wasn't really something. I became more interested by the day, then I learnt HTML, followed by CSS and JavaScript.

After making websites for about 4 months, I learnt C++ and then Python. Following that, I made a couple of casual programs, but eventually I got bored of making desktop apps.

And then I found my love and passion, which was Machine Learning,
At first it was quite painful, because unlike the rest, it required a lot of math. And since I'm just a 16 y.o, I kinda suffered a bit there. But I didn't give up, and I had to learn all that I need from advanced statistics, probability, and calculus; all the good stuff. And then I got to the fun part, which was coding.
The first couple of months were pretty hard, but as always, I didn't give up. I carried on learning and started participating in Data Science competitions as well as using random datasets on the internet to perform EDA on them. At this point, after a lot of hard work and quite a lot of pain, I remain at the rank of 83 out of 2706 competitors in a Data Science competition hosted by Driven Data
